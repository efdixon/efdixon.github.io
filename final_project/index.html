Growing up

Younger Years

Born in Fort Smith, Arkansas in the early 90s, I was born as the third child and second daughter to young parents, Keith and Elizabeth. My brother was 5 years old and my sister had just turned three 10 days before I was born. We moved up to Fayetteville, Arkansas when I was just two years old.

Teen Angst and University

I went to junior high school and ditched soccer for volleyball. Our uniforms were from the early 90s and included a collard sleeveless shirt and basketball shorts that were a size to big for most of us. I met people from schools outside my middle school and elementary years and began to pursue the arts. Once I got to highschool, I kept with volleyball until senior year where I ditched volleyball and went back to soccer where I ended up tearing my ACL senior year.I went to the University of Arkansas in my hometown where I spent 4 years switching 3 majors and eventually graduating with a bachelors degree in Early Childhood Development.

Modern Day

Currently, I live in Washington, DC. The city is quite a change from the homeliness of small town Arkansas, but offers so much in the way of experiences, music, food, food, and more food.
